About

I am a Professor in the Departments of Astronomy and Physics at the University of Arizona, based at Steward Observatory in Tucson. Before joining the University of Arizona, I was a Research Scientist at JPL/Caltech and a postdoctoral researcher at The Ohio State University and the University of Pennsylvania. I received my PhD in Astronomy and Diplom in Physics from the University of Bonn, Germany.

My research focuses on observational and theoretical cosmology — specifically on analyzing data from cosmological surveys to explore fundamental physics questions such as the growth of structures in our Universe, the nature of dark energy and dark matter, and the validity of General Relativity as a description of gravity.

My research group is part of the broader Arizona Cosmology Lab. We are deeply involved in developing the modeling and analysis frameworks of ongoing and future surveys, such as the Dark Energy Survey, the Dark Energy Spectroscopic Instrument, the NASA SPHEREx explorer mission, Rubin Observatory's Legacy Survey of Space and Time, and the Nancy Grace Roman Space Telescope.
